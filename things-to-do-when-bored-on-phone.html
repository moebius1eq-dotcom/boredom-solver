Things to Do When Bored on Your Phone

Your phone is arguably the greatest boredom-fighting tool ever created. It's always in your hand, always connected, and always full of infinite content to scroll through. But let's be honest—doomscrolling the same social media feeds gets old. You need new rabbit holes.

BoredomSolver has compiled the ultimate list of phone activities for when you need to waste some time. These are specifically designed for your handheld device. No productivity, no learning, no self-improvement. Just pure digital procrastination.

Social Media Deep Dives

Beyond the usual Instagram and TikTok feeds, there's a whole world of weird content waiting:

  • Twitter/X Lists – Find lists of weird accounts. Follow them all.
  • Reddit Subreddits – r/Glitch_in_the_Matrix, r/Backrooms, r/123therestaurant
  • TikTok niche content – Search for the most specific things imaginable.
  • Instagram close friends – See what your weird friends post privately.
  • YouTube Shorts rabbit holes – Start with one. You'll emerge 3 hours later.
  • Facebook Groups – Find the weirdest ones. They're out there.

Content Discovery Apps

Sometimes you need to find new things to consume:

  • Podcast apps – Browse by "weird" or "obscure" categories.
  • Curated news apps – Get news from weird sources.
  • Book reading apps – Read the first chapter of random books.
  • Meditation apps – Browse without actually meditating.
  • Language apps – Download lessons you'll never do.

Communication Chaos

When you're bored on your phone, why not mess with your contacts? (Respectfully):

  • Rate your emojis – Go through your keyboard and rate each one.
  • Text yourself – Create a note to future you.
  • Change contact photos – To something hilarious.
  • Make a contact for yourself – Give yourself a weird name.
  • Scroll old texts – Remember when you used to talk to people?

Media Consumption

Watch, read, and listen to things you'll forget immediately:

  • YouTube video essays – 2-hour videos about random topics.
  • Fanfiction – Find the weirdest crossover imaginable.
  • Webtoons – Read the weirdest comics available.
  • Audio dramas – Find the most obscure ones.
  • Music deep dives – Let Spotify's algorithm take you somewhere new.

Phone Organization (Pointless)

Organize things that don't need organizing:

  • Screenshot organization – Delete the 847 screenshots you have.
  • App folder creation – Make folders with weird names.
  • Contact organization – Delete people you don't talk to.
  • Calendar review – Look at dates from 3 years ago.
  • Note organization – Read notes you forgot you wrote.

Research (Useless)

Learn things you'll never need to know:

  • Wikipedia rabbit holes – Start with something random.
  • Celebrity net worth – Find the most random celebrities.
  • Historical events – Read about things that happened on your birthday.
  • Animal facts – Learn about the weirdest animals.
  • Conspiracy theories – Don't believe them, just read them.

Game Apps (Pointless)

Play games that require zero skill:

  • Word games – Scrabble, Wordle, whatever.
  • Match games – Match things. Very engaging.
  • Clicker games – Watch numbers go up.
  • Idle games – Games that play themselves.
  • Trivia games – Get answers wrong on purpose.

Shopping (Looking Only)

Browse without buying:

  • Etsy weirdness – Find the strangest handmade items.
  • Amazon weird products – Sort by weird reviews.
  • Wish.com – Just... look at things.
  • Real estate – Look at houses you'll never afford.
  • Car shopping – Find the most impractical vehicles.

Embrace the Scroll

Here's the truth: sometimes the best thing you can do when bored on your phone is exactly what you're already doing—scrolling. But why not make it more interesting? Try one of these activities instead of your usual doomscroll. Your brain might thank you. Or it won't. Either way, time will pass.

Need more ideas? Check out things to do when bored at home or fun 5-minute activities.

Remember: your phone is a tool for wasting time. Use it wisely.
